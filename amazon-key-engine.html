The brief I set myself

Amazon Key's mission is "seamless, authorized access to doors worldwide." The marketing job is to make that feel effortless, at volume.

Key serves two audiences: homeowners and renters (in-garage delivery, smart locks) and the businesses that manage access at scale (Key for Business, multifamily, builders). That is a lot of copy, across many channels, that all has to sound like one calm, trustworthy brand. Doing it by hand does not scale, and letting a model write unsupervised goes off-brand. The approach here is a system: encode the voice, let the agent draft, score every output, and keep a writer at the gate.

Deliverable 1 · Brand guidelines

The Amazon Key voice, written so a model can follow it

Brand guidelines only matter if they are usable. Each principle below is paired with the behavior it produces in copy, which is how it gets encoded into the agent in the next section.

Brand promise

"Access, handled. Amazon Key gives you secure, authorized access on your terms, so the door is one less thing to think about."

1

Reassuring, never alarming

We sell calm and control, not fear. We never lean on theft scares, "porch pirates," or surveillance language. Security is a quiet promise, not a threat.

2

Effortless & human

Short sentences. Plain words. Second person. If a smart-home skeptic wouldn't say it out loud, we don't write it. No jargon, no hype.

3

Quietly confident

We state benefits plainly and let specifics do the convincing. No superlatives, no "revolutionary." Confidence is shown, not announced.

4

Specific over vague

"Your garage opens just long enough for the package, then closes" beats "super secure." Concrete mechanics build more trust than adjectives ever will.

Messaging pillars

Security you don't think about

Strong, layered, and working in the background, so peace of mind is the default rather than a setting.

Access on your terms

You decide who, when, and how. Turn it on, turn it off, watch it happen. Control never leaves your hands.

Built into the Amazon you trust

Not another gadget to manage. It is part of the delivery and devices experience customers already use.

Lexicon (the part the model gets wrong most)

✓ Words we use

  • authorized access · on your terms
  • securely · verified driver · time-limited
  • you're in control · turn it on or off anytime
  • delivered inside · off the porch
  • watch it happen · get a notification

✕ Words we avoid

  • porch pirates · theft · stolen
  • 100% safe · never worry · guaranteed
  • revolutionary · cutting-edge · game-changing
  • unlock your home to strangers
  • seamless · ultimate · unleash

Compliance guardrails (non-negotiable): no absolute security or safety guarantees; no claims about device compatibility, price, or availability unless supplied in the brief; always preserve the customer's control and choice in the message. These become the agent's hard rules.

The agent's system prompt
# SYSTEM: "Amazon Key Copywriter" You are the Amazon Key Copywriter. You draft marketing copy that a human editor reviews before anything ships. Your job is on-brand drafts, not final word. VOICE (always): • Reassuring, never alarming. Sell calm and control, not fear. Never invoke theft, "porch pirates," or surveillance. • Effortless & human. Short sentences, plain words, second person. Banned hype: revolutionary, cutting-edge, seamless, game-changing, ultimate, unleash. • Quietly confident. State benefits plainly; let specifics convince. • Specific over vague. Prefer concrete mechanics to adjectives. HARD RULES (never violate): • No absolute guarantees ("100% safe", "never worry again", "guaranteed"). Security is strong, layered, and customer-controlled. Say that instead. • No unverified claims. Don't assert device compatibility, price, or availability unless given in FACTS. If missing, write [VERIFY: …]. • Always preserve customer control in the message. • Match the requested format: length, structure, and CTA style. OUTPUT (JSON): { "headline":"", "subhead":"", "body":"", "cta":"", "self_eval": { "voice":1-5, "accuracy":1-5, "compliance_flags":[] } } Before returning, score your own draft and surface any flags for the reviewer.

Deliverable 3 · Quality evaluation framework

Every draft is scored before a human ever reads it

Measuring AI output against creative standards only works if the standard is written down. This is the rubric the eval agent and the human editor both use, with the same six dimensions and the same bar.

DimensionWhat a 5 looks likeBar
Brand-voice adherenceSounds like Key: reassuring, plain, specific. No banned or hype words.
The dimension models fail most.
≥ 4
Message accuracyEvery factual claim is supported by the brief's FACTS. No invented features, specs, or numbers.= 5
Compliance & claims safetyNo absolute guarantees, no privacy overreach, customer control preserved. Any risk is flagged.= 5
Format fitHits the channel's length, structure, and CTA conventions exactly. A push reads like a push.≥ 4
Clarity & concisionOne idea per line. Nothing a reader has to re-read. Cut 20% and it still works.≥ 4
Hook & CTA strengthOpens on the customer's moment; the action is obvious and singular.≥ 4

The pass gate

A draft is auto-routed to quick approve only if accuracy and compliance score 5, every other dimension is ≥ 4, and there are zero open flags. Anything else goes to full editorial review. Nothing ships unscored.

The feedback loop

When the editor's score disagrees with the agent's self-score, that gap is the signal. Recurring misses (for example, the model keeps reaching for "seamless") become new lexicon rules and few-shot examples. These are versioned, so voice quality can be tracked over time.

LLM-as-judge: eval prompt (excerpt)
# EVAL: score a draft against the Key rubric Score the DRAFT 1-5 on each rubric dimension. For voice and compliance, quote the exact span that loses points and name the rule it breaks. Return: { scores:{…}, flags:[…], verdict:"approve|review|reject", top_fix:"the single highest-impact edit" } # A draft that says "100% safe" → compliance:1, flag:"absolute guarantee", # verdict:"reject", even if every other score is perfect.

B2CProduct detail page

In-garage delivery: product page

Brief: Convert shoppers browsing in-garage delivery. Audience: homeowners with a compatible garage. Make it feel calm and concrete.

✕ Raw AI draft
Never Worry About Package Theft Again! With Amazon Key, your packages are 100% safe and secure, every single time. Say goodbye to porch pirates forever and enjoy total peace of mind with the most advanced delivery technology available. It's revolutionary, seamless, and completely changes the way you receive deliveries. Buy Now
✓ Shipped (edited)
Your packages, delivered inside your garage. Amazon Key lets your driver place deliveries securely in your garage, off the porch and out of the weather before you're even home. How it works
  • Watch it happen. Get a notification when delivery is on the way, and again once it's inside, with a video clip if you have a compatible camera.
  • You're always in control. Open or close your garage from the app, and turn delivery access on or off anytime.
  • Works with the garage you have. Connects to compatible myQ-enabled openers, so there's no new hardware to buy.
See if your garage is compatible
✍ Editor's note

Three problems. (1) Absolute guarantees. "100% safe," "never worry again," and "forever" are claims we can't make, and the rubric rejects them. (2) Fear and hype. "Porch pirates," "revolutionary," and "seamless" break the voice and read as anxious and salesy. (3) No substance. The reader never learns how it works. The edit replaces the hype with the mechanics, and swaps "Buy Now" for the real next step, a compatibility check.

B2CLifecycle email

First in-garage delivery: onboarding email

Brief: Reassure a new user before their first garage delivery, which is the highest-anxiety moment in the journey. Set expectations and hand over control.

✕ Raw AI draft
Subject Your Amazon Key delivery is coming! 🎉 Hi there! Thanks for signing up for Amazon Key. Your first in-garage delivery is on its way. Our technology is super secure, so you don't need to worry about anything. Sit back, relax, and let us handle everything for you. We're so excited to have you! Learn More
✓ Shipped (edited)
Subject What to expect with your first garage delivery Preheader You're in control the whole way through. Hi Mridhula,

Your first in-garage delivery is set for Thursday. Here's exactly how it goes:
  1. We text you when your driver is about 10 minutes away.
  2. Your garage opens just long enough for the package, then closes on its own.
  3. You get a confirmation, and a video clip if you have a compatible camera, the moment it's done.
Prefer the porch this time? Switch off garage delivery for any order in the app, anytime. Open the Key app
✍ Editor's note

The draft's "super secure, don't worry" actually raises anxiety: it names the fear without answering it, at the moment trust is most fragile. It also centers the company ("we're so excited") instead of the customer. The edit does the one job this email has: walk the customer through the steps and give them the off-switch. The subject line changes from brand hype to a plain promise of clarity.

How I'd run this at Amazon Key

From demo to production: what I'd own and measure

This page is a one-person proof. In my current role I run the production version of exactly this playbook on a live Amazon Ads content platform. Here's how that translates to owning Key's written-content workflow.

First-pass acceptance
% of drafts an editor approves with no edits. Target ramp ~40% to 75% as the feedback loop matures. This is the clearest signal the agent is learning the voice.
Brand-voice score
Rolling average on the 6-dimension rubric. Hold the line at ≥ 4.5 / 5; investigate any channel that drifts.
Editor edit-distance
How much a human changes each draft. Should trend down per format over time; spikes flag a stale prompt or a new product nuance.
Cycle time / asset
Brief to published. The lever for volume and speed, without letting it pull quality down.
Compliance catch-rate
Non-compliant or off-brand lines caught before publish. Target: 100%. This is the guardrail that protects the brand at scale.

I would bring that system to Amazon Key, tuned to its voice and customers.
